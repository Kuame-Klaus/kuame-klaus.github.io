Background

What I do

I'm a Senior Analyst at EY Ghana, working across Assurance and Data & Technology Risk. Most of my engagements involve testing and reviewing enterprise systems, banking cores, and ERPs for financial institutions across Ghana, Nigeria, Rwanda, Malawi, Tanzania, and Sierra Leone.

Outside client work, I spend a lot of time in financial modelling and equity research, building models for M&A scenarios, IPOs, and loan structures, mostly with a Ghanaian and West African context in mind. I'm working toward bridging assurance grade rigor with financial modelling and equity research, with an eye on CFO track and advisory work.

I'm also actively learning IT audit, fintech, forensic accounting and fraud investigations, investing, and new technology, areas I see as a natural extension of the work I already do.

Beyond the Desk

Sankofa Pathways Foundation

This one's still on the drawing board, not yet running, but it's a real plan I'm building alongside my day job. Sankofa Pathways Foundation is a proposed Ghanaian NGO aimed at closing the career-awareness and digital-skills gap for senior high school students in rural Ghana, starting in the Eastern Region.

Named for the Akan principle of Sankofa, learning from the past to build a better future, the proposal outlines three programmes: career awareness campaigns bringing real professionals into schools, digital skills bootcamps, and long-term Rural IT Development Centres. Right now the priority is structuring the financial model and building relationships with founding sponsors and partners to get it off the ground.

Vision: 10 SHS schools/year Vision: 5,000+ students reached/year Vision: 3 Rural IT Centres by Year 3 Status: seeking founding sponsors
